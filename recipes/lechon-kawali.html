Lechon Kawali

Lechon Kawali is a Filipino dish of deep-fried pork belly that's crispy on the outside and tender on the inside.
It's often served with a dipping sauce like sarsa ng litson (lechon sauce) or toyomansi (soy sauce with calamansi).

Originating from the Spanish cooking technique of lechón. It's made by deep-frying pork belly until the skin is crispy and golden, while the meat stays tender and juicy.

Prep Time: 10 mins | Cook Time: 45 mins

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ pounds pork belly, cut into small pieces
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 cups oil for frying, or as needed

Directions

  1. Combine pork belly, garlic, bay leaves, salt, and pepper in a skillet; pour in enough water to cover.
    Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer until skin is tender, 35 to 45 minutes.

  2. Drain and let sit at room temperature until pork has air-dried.

  3. Heat oil in a deep-fryer or large saucepan to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).

  4. Fry pork in the hot oil, working in batches, until golden brown, 3 to 5 minutes.

  5. Remove fried pork with a slotted spoon and drain on a paper towel-lined plate. Serve!
