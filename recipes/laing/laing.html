LAING

Laing is a Filipino dish made with taro leaves cooked in coconut milk.
The dish is spiced with labuyo chili, lemongrass, garlic, shallots, ginger, and shrimp paste.
Laing is often eaten with meat or fish and white rice.

It originates from the Bicol Region, where it is known simply as pinangat.

Prep Time: ~10 mins | Cook Time: ~60 mins

Ingredients

  • 3.5 oz dried taro leaves
  • 6 cups coconut milk
  • 2 cups coconut cream
  • 1 cup shrimp paste or bagoong
  • 0.5 lbs pork shoulder
  • 6 pieces red chili
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 0.5 cups ginger
  • 8 cloves garlic, crushed

Directions

  1. Combine the coconut milk, pork, shrimp paste, ginger, onion, and garlic in a cooking pot.
    Heat the pot and let boil.

  2. Once the mixture starts to boil, gently stir to mix the ingredients.
    Cover the pot and simmer for 15 to 20 minutes.
    Make sure to stir once in a while to prevent ingredients from sticking at the bottom of the pot.

  3. Add the dried taro leaves. Do not stir. Let it stay until the leaves absorb the coconut milk.
    This takes about 20 to 30 minutes.
    You can gently push the leaves down to absorb more coconut milk.

  4. Once the leaves absorb the coconut milk, stir the leaves.
    Continue to cook for to 10 minutes.

  5. Pour the coconut cream into the cooking pot. Add the red chilies. Stir.
    Cook for 10 to 12 minutes. Serve!
